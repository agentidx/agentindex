## Ratio calculation

Per protocol: `citation_rate_kings / citation_rate_nonkings`

```
King rate:     9 / 520 = 0.01731
Non-King rate: 88 / 293,435 = 0.00030
Ratio: 0.01731 / 0.00030 = 57.71x
```

## Verdict (pre-registered thresholds)

- **Ratio: 57.71x**
- **Threshold: ≥3x**
- **Hypothesis: SUPPORTED**

Kings receive AI citations at a rate 57.7x higher per submitted page than non-Kings, even after the experiment equalized submission rates via random sampling. The Kings concept captures something real about AI crawler preferences — it is not primarily submission bias.

## Notes

- **Table swap active**: `agent_jurisdiction_status` table swap was running during the measurement window. This affects compliance pages, not /safe/ pages. No impact on citation measurement.
- **Stability sprint**: Multiple API restarts occurred during the window (stability Dag 1-5). These caused brief (<30s) downtimes but should not systematically bias Kings vs non-Kings citations.
- **Citation attribution**: 959 of 1,056 total citations (90.8%) went to pages NOT in the submitted pool — AI bots independently discover and cite pages beyond what we submit to IndexNow. This suggests IndexNow submission is only one of several discovery channels.
- **Small Kings sample**: Only 520 Kings were submitted (vs 37,688 total Kings). The 9 citations on 520 pages is a small sample. Effect size (57.7x) should be interpreted with appropriate statistical caution.
