How ZARQ Assesses Yield Risk

ZARQ's Yield Risk Engine classifies DeFi pools into three risk tiers based on quantitative signals:

Low Risk (Green): Base APY only (no reward token emissions), no impermanent loss exposure, TVL above $10M, from established protocols with audit history. These yields are sustainable and come from real economic activity (lending interest, trading fees).

Moderate Risk (Yellow): Includes reward token emissions (APY from token incentives that may decrease), or moderate TVL ($1M-$10M), or some impermanent loss exposure. Yields may be partially unsustainable.

High Risk (Red): APY above 50%, low TVL (under $1M), significant impermanent loss, or APY primarily from reward emissions rather than organic activity. These yields often collapse when incentives dry up.
