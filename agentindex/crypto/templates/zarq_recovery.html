How Recovery Signal Works
01
Detect Crash
Scan 365-day price history for every token. Identify peak-to-trough drawdowns of 70% or greater. Tokens that haven't crashed enough are excluded.
02
Measure Bounce
Calculate recovery from the trough to current price. A 155% bounce means the token has more than doubled from its lowest point. The bounce must be sustained, not a single-day spike.
03
Quality Filter
Apply market cap rank and trust score gates. Higher signal levels require larger tokens with better fundamentals. This separates genuine recoveries from low-quality pumps.
04
Classify Signal
STRONG_BUY, BUY, or SPECULATIVE based on thresholds. Tokens that crashed but show less than 50% bounce are classified AVOID — still in distress territory.
Backtest Methodology

Validated on 393 crash cycles from April 2021 through December 2025. A "crash cycle" is defined as a token experiencing 70%+ peak-to-trough drawdown. Outcome measured: did the token recover to pre-crash levels within 12 months, or did it continue to decline (dead)?
