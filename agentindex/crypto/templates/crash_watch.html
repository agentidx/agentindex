How ZARQ Predicts Structural Collapse

ZARQ's crash model combines Distance-to-Default (DtD) scoring with 7 quantitative signals to detect structural weakness before price collapse. The model is adapted from Merton's structural credit framework, originally used to predict corporate defaults. When a token's DtD falls below 1.0, it has breached the distress threshold — historically, this has preceded terminal failure with 100% accuracy.

The 7 signals: Liquidity Depth (10%), Holder Concentration (5%), Ecosystem Resilience (30%), Fundamental Activity (10%), Contagion Exposure (25%), Structural Risk (5%), Relative Weakness (15%). Each is calibrated against historical collapses to maximize predictive power.
